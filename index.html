What it does

One platform for the two numbers airlines cannot ignore: cost and carbon.

Fuel is 25 to 30% of operating cost, and since January 2025 the EU ETS requires monitoring of non-CO₂ effects. GreenWings handles both in one grounded, human-in-the-loop workflow.

The surprise lever

Contrails warm more than all of aviation's CO₂.

Contrail cirrus radiative forcing is 57.4 versus 34.3 mW/m² for CO₂ (Lee et al. 2021). In the American Airlines and Google trial, small altitude changes on about 2% of flights cut contrail formation 54 to 64%, at roughly 0.3% fuel cost. GreenWings surfaces where that lever pays off.
