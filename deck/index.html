The problem

It is not just CO₂.

Aviation is one of the hardest sectors to decarbonise, and its climate impact is broader than fuel burn alone.

  • Contrail cirrus warms more than all of aviation's CO₂ (radiative forcing 57.4 vs 34.3 mW/m², Lee et al. 2021).
  • Add NOₓ, water vapour and soot, and non-CO₂ effects are roughly two-thirds of the impact.
  • Count CO₂ only, and you are managing about a third of the real problem.
The gap

Teams have data. They lack a decision.

  • Calculators, fuel platforms and ESG reports all answer one question: what happened.
  • None answer the Monday-morning question: what should we do next?
  • Stakeholders: airline sustainability & operations teams, regulators, the flying public.
  • Aligned to UN SDGs 13, 9 and 12 - and it is a true AI problem: weighing climate, cost, feasibility and operations across thousands of flights.
Why now

Three forces are converging.

Regulation

Since 2025 the EU ETS requires monitoring of non-CO₂ effects - exactly what GreenWings computes.

Operational proof

Airline × Google trials show small altitude changes cut contrail warming by more than half.

Governed AI

Enterprises now demand AI that is auditable and accountable, not a black box.

The market is moving from carbon reporting to governed climate decisions - now.

Positioning

Not another calculator. The decision layer.

  • Existing tools generate figures. GreenWings orchestrates them into decisions.
  • The only option that is non-CO₂-aware, recommends ranked actions, and keeps a human in control.
  • We sit on top of existing tools, not in place of them.
The product · Decision Copilot

An orchestrator, not a chatbot.

Select scenario→ Validate coverage→ Estimate footprint→ Run scenarios→ Rank actions→ Human review→ Decision brief

Data in, decision brief out. A controlled sequence of trusted tools with a review gate - each recommendation scored on climate, cost, feasibility and disruption.

Architecture

The AI never invents numbers.

  • Every quantitative output comes from a deterministic tool over real data.
  • The language model only orchestrates and explains - a strict tool allowlist.
  • It declines when data does not support an answer.
  • The flexibility of an AI agent with the reliability of audited software.
Reliability, trust & governance

Governance is designed in.

  • Hallucination: the AI sources no numbers - grounded by architecture.
  • Bias & fairness: feasibility-scored levers; no penalty for developing markets.
  • Evaluation: figures checked vs source; supplied vs modelled labelled; uncertainty shown.
  • Privacy & accountability: aggregate data (GDPR-light); named human approver; EU AI Act decision-support tier.

Social & economic

Ethical
